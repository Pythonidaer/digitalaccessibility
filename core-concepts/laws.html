Laws (ADA, Section 508)

Notes from the former Foundations page: the ADA vs. Section 508 comparison and the Violations section.

ADA vs. Section 508

  • ADA (Americans with Disabilities Act) is a broad civil rights law prohibiting discrimination based on disability in public life.
  • Section 508 is a federal mandate that specifically requires federal electronic & information technology to be accessible.

Both align with W.C.A.G. standards, but A.D.A. applies more broadly (e.g., wheelchair ramps, public transportation, etc.), while Section 508 is specific to federal agencies and their digital content.

Violations

Violations depend on the entity. If it's a federal agency or someone contracting with the government, failing to provide accessible digital content would violate Section 508. If it's a private public accomodation, the A.D.A. would be the law in question.

Both, however, aim for digital accessibility, so the standards (like W.C.A.G.) are often the same.
