Audience Lists

These categories help tailor information for specialists.

Specialists need deep standards, creators need practical guides, & leadership needs summaries.

  • Specialists/Practioners:
    • Digital Accessibility Specialists
    • Web Accessibility Managers
    • UX Designers
    • Compliance Officers
    • This group needs in-depth standards, polices, and audit tools
  • Content Creators:
    • Faculty
    • Staff
    • Editors
    • These individuals need practical guidelines on accessible documents, videos, and web content.
  • Developers/IT:
    • Software Engineers
    • IT staff
    • This group needs coding standards (ARIA, W.C.A.G. integration) and testing procedures.
  • Leadership/Legal:
    • Adminstrators
    • Legal Counsel
    • These stakeholders need high-level policy summaries, risk assessments, and progress reports.
  • Users with Disabilities:
    • End users with vision, hearing, cognitive, motor disabilities
    • While not internal, keep them in mind for feedback, usability testing, and ensuring content meets their needs

Roles and Departments

Roles and departments vary based on the organization. They are typically:

  • Content Editors and faculty focus on document and media accessibility (like alt text and captions).
  • IT often ensure systems (like Content Management Systems) support accessibility features.
  • Software Engineers handle the code-level compliance (such as ARIA attributes).
  • Digital Accessibility Speciailists may bridge all these, guiding testing and remediation.

For presenting information, you can segment by role—give editors practical guides on media, engineers technical criteria, and IT and specialists broader standards.

Each team plays its part.
