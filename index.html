Quiz Questions

  • What is one key technical standard I'd be assessing content against as a Digital Accessibility Specialist?
    • I would be assessing content against the WCAG standards, which covers the 4 Principles of Accessibility: Perceivable, Operable, Understandable, and Robust.
  • What content types would I be expected to audit and remediate for accessibility in this role?
    • I would be expected to audit and remediate for accessibility in the following content types: websites, mobile and desktop apps, video tutorials, and digital documents.

Platform-Specific Tools

iOS

  • VoiceOver and test whether all elements are spoken and operable by swiping.
    • Use iOS Accessibility Inspector to check labels, traits, and contrast.

Desktop

  • Keyboard navigation, screen readers like NVDA or JAWS, or built-in accessibility tools.
  • In both cases > check if all interactive elements are properly labeled, focusable, and properly ordered

Scope

  • (Potentially) providing input on writing
  • Collaborating with content or marketing
  • Identify readability issues for complex teams
    • Then work with content creators to simplify it
  • Provide guidance on plain language
    • But the final wording comes from those teams
  • Be a partner in the process

Certifications

  • Earning the following shows you have formal training in accessibility
  • CPACC
    • The exam takes about 2 hours
    • With 100 multiple-choice questions
    • $510 for non-members ($410 for members)
  • WAS
    • Also 2 hours
    • With 75 multiple-choice questions
    • $555 for non-members ($455 for members)
  • Both are from the International Association of Accessibility Professionals (IAAP)
    • Passing both get you the CPWA certification
  • You study indepdently using recommended materials
    • You then register for an online proctored exam, pass it, then earn the certification
